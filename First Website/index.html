GAMING IS NOT A CRIME



The debate as to whether video games are good or bad for us has been going since the days of retro games such as Pong and Space Invaders.

Unfortunately, most people have always assumed that they were the cause of many mental and physical problems. Even there was a lack of scientific evidence to prove it.


Some say it is in our human nature to find something to blame when there is an issue, and video games have always been an easy target.


But these things have changed and now more professional studies are being conducted to find out the truth.


Are video games good for you?


Or, do video games really hinder our ability to learn, make us more violent, or affect our physical health?


While there isn't enough evidence to offer a solid answer, the good news is that most researcher are discovering that games can be good for us.


This article will show you 10 reasons why video games are good for you.


Before you read further remember that anything in exceess can be harmful .


No benefit that comes from a video game will justify playing them for 10 hours a day.


It's ones responsibility to figure out how long you should play before putting the controller down and doing something else, as difficult as it may be


Video games are often blamed for being a bad influence and a big distraction for children and teens alike, but are they really all that horrible?


It all depends on how parents monitor the games that there kids are allowed to play and teaching them about the difference between fiction and reality.


There are plenty of adults today that still play games. They claim that it keeps there mind fresh, helps them be more alert in real life, and keeps depression at bay. Here are 10 reasons why video games deserve the benifit of the doubt.


TEN REASONS WHY VIDEO GAMES ARE GOOD FOR YOU


1. Improves your vision




First-person shooting games such as "Call of Duty" and "Far Cry" can actually improve your visual ability called contrast sensitivity function. This ability allows people to detect even subtle changes in shades of gray against a uniformly coloured backdrop, which is very important for reading and driving at night. However, contrast sensitivity decreases as you age. So exercising it with first-person on other action based games actually helps to prevent it from happening.



2. Could slow down aging




Many of my relatives who's around his mid 50's is still playing video games till this day. Science has shown that individuals from ages 50 and older who play video games have an improved or stabilized mental state. Video games such as "World of Warcraft" are complex and requires you to use your mental energy. It helps to exercise your brain which will be better for you in the long run.


3. Pain and Stress reliver




Studies have shoen that children and adults who are recovering from painful medical procedures felt less painn when thay were distracted bt video games. Playing video games helped to let their brain focus on other things instead of on their aching spots. It is also proven that puzzle video games such as "Angry Birds" and "Bejewelled" can improves players' moods, promote relacation, and ward off anxiety.


4. Improves problem solving skills




Strategy games that makes you scratch your head to solve its puzzeles can improve your solving skills. Real-time stategy games especially can help improve your overall cognitive flexibility in normal taskss. Video games can challenge your brain, but sometimes can frustrate you when you can't solve a level. Don't take the game too seriously, after all it's just a game.


5. Encourages physical activity




Whether you're a fan of "Fifa 2015" or the interactive games on the Nintendo Wii and Xbox Kinect, these somehoe can get you off the couch to go and play the real thing. Studies have found that adolescents that play sports-themed video games will feel more intrested and encouraged to try the sports in real life


What encourages them to try the sports in real-life is the self-esteem and some sports knowledge gained from the sports themed games. The interavtive games on the Nintendo, Wii, Playstation VR and Xbox Kinect can help you break a sweat while you are competing with someone at the same time.


6. Aids children with dyslexia to read better




Researchers have found that children with dyslexia who played action video games such as "Rayman RavingRabids" and racing games such as "Forza Horizon" for 12 hours are able to read better then the ones who didn't play video games. This is because action video games help to improve your attention skills making it easier for you to read faster and better


7. Boosts your creativity




Another health benifit that "Starcraft" can provide to gamers is a more creative thinking. Fastpaced strategy games encourages players to think about strategies faster and improve their multitasking abilitys. People who play video games are more creative than people who play less because it increases your brain possibility to think deep and open the doors of imagination of brain which were closed before.


8. Improves your social skills




Children who play more friendly or pro-social games tend to have a happier outlook. Somehow they will be more sociable, helpful and less likely to behave badly in social situations. Multiplayer games like "Counter Strike Global Offence(CS-GO)", "Players Unknown Battleground(PUBG)", "Defence of the Ancients(Dota)" and "League of Legends(LOL)" somehow teaches player to work in teams, make decisions and build qualities such as leadership. That's why you have world tournaments for these games because different people can work together with the aim to win the game.


9. Helps control cravings




What video games have is the power of distraction. People who chose to play "Tetris" have less cravings while people who chose to wait for a computer program to load has a strong craving. For example, if you're trying to stop smoking, we suggest you turn to video game before you succumb to your craving for a smoke somehow it will help a lot.


10. Makes your hand-eye coordination better




Gamers who have played games like "Assassin's Creed" and "Tomb Raider" franchise might have a better hand-eye coordination than non-gamers.That's not to say that non-gamers can't be better but because gamers are trained when they play action games, they learn and perform faster. Also, surgeons who play video games made less mistakes and performed faster in laparoscopic surgery then those who didn't play games.
